The Research Question

ONE ASSUMPTION TO TEST

The Assumption (Going In)

Mita Residential assumed that longer guest stays generate more concierge revenue. The logic: more nights = more time to use services = higher spend. This had never been tested with data.

What We Actually Found

Nights stayed has near-zero effect on revenue (R² ≈ 0, p=0.90). The assumption was wrong. What drives revenue is entirely different: which services guests book, not how long they stay.

Key Findings

SERVICES DRIVE EVERYTHING

+$80K
Activities Package

Guests who booked Activities generated $80K more revenue than those who didn't. Only 25% current uptake — meaning 75% of high-value guests are walking past the highest-ROI service.

+$70K
Spa Services

Spa bookings add $70K in incremental revenue with only 16% current uptake. The lowest adoption rate among premium services, representing the largest untapped opportunity.

+$68K
Cribs & Extra Beds

Family accommodation add-ons drive $68K uplift at 14% uptake. Signals a family guest segment that is underserved and under-marketed to in current acquisition strategy.

Gateway
Chef & Transport

Chef services and airport transport show lower direct revenue contribution but correlate strongly with multi-service bookings. These are gateway services — entry points that predict higher total spend.

Model Summary

Final model: R² = 0.505 (services-only specification). The null model with nights stayed only: R² ≈ 0. Service type explains over 50% of concierge revenue variation. Nights stayed explains essentially none.

Business Recommendations

4 ACTIONS FOR MITA

01
PRIORITIZE ACTIVITIES + SPA AT BOOKING
↑ $80K + $70K potential per booking cohort

Move Activities and Spa from optional add-ons to default inclusions in the booking flow. Present as curated experiences, not upsells. Current 25% and 16% uptake is a funnel problem, not a product problem.

02
STOP MARKETING EXTENDED STAYS AS A REVENUE LEVER
Redirect budget to service conversion

The data is unambiguous: nights stayed has zero revenue impact. Any marketing spend targeting "longer stay = more value" is misallocated. Shift messaging to experience richness, not duration.

03
BUILD A FAMILY SEGMENT ACQUISITION STRATEGY
↑ $68K from Cribs/Extra Beds segment

Families booking Cribs and Extra Beds are high-value guests. Create targeted family packages that bundle accommodation with Activities and Chef service — natural complements for families with children.

04
USE CHEF + TRANSPORT AS GATEWAY SERVICES
Correlates with multi-service bookings

These lower-revenue services are strong predictors of total booking value. Offer them as low-friction first steps — complimentary airport pickup, free first dinner — to trigger multi-service booking behavior.

Reflection

WHAT THE DATA TAUGHT ME

"The most valuable finding wasn't what drove revenue —
it was disproving what everyone assumed did."

Walking into this project, Mita's team was optimizing for longer stays. The data said: stop. That kind of finding — the one that contradicts the operating assumption — is worth ten times more than confirming what you already believe.

This project reinforced something I carry from P&G: good strategy starts with honest measurement. Before you optimize anything, you have to know what's actually moving the needle. In Mita's case, the answer was services — and the opportunity was hiding in plain sight.
